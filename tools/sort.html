Surgical severity grades
Minor (coefficient 0): excision of skin lesion; drainage of breast abscess.
Intermediate (coefficient 0): primary repair of inguinal hernia; excision of varicose veins; tonsillectomy; knee arthroscopy.
Major (coefficient 0): total abdominal hysterectomy; transurethral resection of prostate (TURP); lumbar discectomy; thyroidectomy.
Xmajor / complex (coefficient +0.381): total joint replacement; lung operations; colonic resection; radical neck dissection; and major vascular (e.g. aortic aneurysm), oesophagectomy, or pancreatectomy.

*Severity is graded by the AXA / BUPA specialist procedure codes (SORT's original definition; the two schedules differ for only ~3.5% of procedures, and never by more than one category). The examples above are representative items from the concordant NICE grades of surgery (NG45), for quick reference only. In the SORT model only Xmajor/complex carries +0.381; the other three grades are the reference baseline (0).
